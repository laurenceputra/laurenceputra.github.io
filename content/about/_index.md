Laurence Putra Franslay is an engineering leader with a strong engineering and operations background. He has worked on both sides of the dev and ops world as an engineer and as a manager, and he is known for being able to pick up new technologies quickly when the problem demands it.

Today, Laurence serves as Site Leader for the Server Architecture org in TikTok and runs the eSRE function in TikTok Singapore. His broader background spans mobile, web, servers, infrastructure, DevOps, and engineering management.

## Engineering

Laurence's earlier CV described him as a versatile engineer with experience across software engineering, infrastructure, mobile, web, and operations. He has led teams, handled platform migrations, improved latency, modernized infrastructure, and still values staying hands-on when needed.

A recurring theme in his work is that strong fundamentals matter more than chasing fashionable tools. That belief shows up in both the way he builds teams and the way he solves technical problems.

## Community work

Laurence has dedicated significant time to social empowerment and community building.

From 2014 to 2024, he contributed to IAmTalentedSG, improving operational efficiency with technology while also serving as the event photographer. The initiative focused on uplifting youths in at-risk communities.

During the COVID-19 crisis, Laurence co-founded Project Stable Staples with a small team responding to the needs of families living in rental communities. He helped launch the donation portal in under two days, shaped donor engagement and public communications, and helped the initiative raise more than SGD 160,000. That support reached more than 2,600 individuals across more than 600 households.

Even after the peak pandemic period, the work continued at a smaller and more sustainable scale so families in transition still had a rapid support system until existing social infrastructure could take over.

## Philanthropy and mentoring

Outside direct on-the-ground work, Laurence has also supported philanthropic efforts such as the Lai Yu Hua Bursary. His approach is not just to fund support, but to stay engaged with recipients and help make the support meaningful.

He has also spent years contributing to broader communities, from GeekcampSG and NUSHackers in his student years to mentoring and alumni engagement through the National University of Singapore.

## How he works

Laurence tends to work from first principles. He values resilient teams, practical execution, and using whatever skills are most useful in the moment. In community work, that can mean logistics, communications, fundraising, or building tools quickly. In engineering, it means combining leadership with enough technical depth to stay credible and effective.
