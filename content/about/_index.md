Laurence Putra Franslay is an engineering leader with deep engineering and operations experience. He has worked across development and operations as both an engineer and a manager, and he picks up new technologies quickly when the problem demands it.

Today, Laurence serves as Site Leader for the Server Architecture org in TikTok and runs the eSRE function in TikTok Singapore. His broader background spans mobile, web, servers, infrastructure, DevOps, and engineering management.

## Engineering

Laurence is a versatile engineer with experience across software engineering, infrastructure, mobile, web, and operations. He has led teams, driven platform migrations, reduced latency, modernized infrastructure, and stayed hands-on when needed.

Strong fundamentals matter more than fashionable tools. That belief shapes both how he builds teams and how he solves technical problems.

## Community work

Laurence has dedicated significant time to social empowerment and community building.

From 2014 to 2024, he contributed to IAmTalentedSG, used technology to improve operational efficiency, and served as the event photographer. The initiative focused on uplifting youths in at-risk communities.

During the COVID-19 crisis, Laurence co-founded Project Stable Staples with a small team responding to the needs of families living in rental communities. He launched the donation portal in under two days, shaped donor engagement and public communications, and drove a campaign that raised more than SGD 160,000. That support reached more than 2,600 individuals across more than 600 households.

After the peak pandemic period, the work continued at a smaller and more sustainable scale so families in transition still had a rapid support system until existing social infrastructure could take over.

## Philanthropy and mentoring

Outside direct on-the-ground work, Laurence supports philanthropic efforts such as the Lai Yu Hua Bursary. He does not just fund support. He stays engaged with recipients and works to make that support meaningful.

He has spent years contributing to broader communities, from GeekcampSG and NUSHackers in his student years to mentoring and alumni engagement through the National University of Singapore.

## How he works

Laurence works from first principles. He values resilient teams, practical execution, and using whatever skills are most useful in the moment. In community work, that can mean logistics, communications, fundraising, or building tools quickly. In engineering, it means combining leadership with enough technical depth to stay credible and effective.
